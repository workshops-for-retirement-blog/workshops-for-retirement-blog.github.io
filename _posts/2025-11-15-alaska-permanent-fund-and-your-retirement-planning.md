## What Is the Alaska Permanent Fund?

The Alaska Permanent Fund is a state-run investment fund established to share Alaska’s oil wealth with residents. Each year, qualifying residents receive a dividend—often several thousand dollars. While it’s a welcome boost, it’s just one piece of a comprehensive retirement strategy.

## Should You Count on the Alaska Permanent Fund in Retirement?

The Permanent Fund Dividend (PFD) can be a helpful supplement, but it shouldn’t be your sole plan. Here are a few reasons why:

- **Amounts Can Fluctuate:** Annual payments change, so they don’t offer a fixed income.
- **Not a Replacement for Social Security:** It’s a bonus, not a primary retirement benefit.
- **Works Best with a Plan:** Combining it with smart investment advice for seniors, tax strategies for retirees, and a solid Social Security claiming plan makes all the difference.
