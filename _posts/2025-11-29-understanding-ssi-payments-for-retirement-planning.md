## What Are SSI Payments and Who Qualifies?

SSI payments are a federal program designed to help seniors and individuals with disabilities who have limited income and resources. For retirees, understanding how SSI might supplement your income—especially if you haven’t paid into Social Security for enough years—can be an important part of your overall financial strategy.

**Key facts about SSI payments:**
- SSI is different from Social Security retirement benefits.
- Eligibility is based on age, disability status, and financial need.
- SSI can help cover basic living expenses for those who qualify.

But SSI payments are just one piece of the retirement puzzle. Integrating SSI with Social Security, pension income, retirement savings, and other benefits requires careful planning.

## How SSI Payments Fit Into Retirement Planning

When considering your retirement income, it’s helpful to see the big picture. SSI payments may work alongside other sources like Social Security, 401(k) withdrawals, and investments. But maximizing your benefits—while minimizing taxes and protecting your estate—can get complicated.
